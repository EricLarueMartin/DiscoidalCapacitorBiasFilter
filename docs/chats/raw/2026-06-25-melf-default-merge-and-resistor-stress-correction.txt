Resistor voltage-stress correction:

- Do not size the MELF or resistive-core path as if each resistor must hold the full 6 kV detector-bias supply in normal operation.
- Detector load is below 1 nA, so resistor voltage drop is approximately `I * R`, not the full supply voltage.
- Full-bias breakdown remains a dielectric/spacing issue and a pulse-energy issue. If a breakdown happens, the available energy is approximately `0.5 C V^2`, usually with pF-scale capacitance in this geometry.
- Resistors should still be chosen with low noise, leakage/surface tracking, pulse robustness, potting compatibility, and local field geometry in mind.
