Official Vishay MMB 0207 professional-family data used for first-pass sizing:

- Resistance range: up to 15 Mohm for MMB 0207.
- 10 Mohm is available in the 50 ppm/K, 1% range.
- The 25 ppm/K, 0.5% MMB 0207 range only reaches 1 Mohm.
- Rated operating voltage: 350 V AC RMS/DC per resistor.
- Rated dissipation: 1 W in power operation, 0.4 W in standard low-drift operation, provided film-temperature limits are maintained.
- Body size: about 5.8 mm long by 2.2 mm diameter.

Additional user note later the same day:

- User wants the web interface to retain a record of another option besides the ferrite/resistive-core approach.
- Candidate part: 0207 MELF resistor `MMB0207MC1005FB200`.
- Length is just under 6 mm, with about 3.2 mm between the end caps.
- The user sees a 1.5 mm thick plate and washer stack as mechanically plausible for this part.
- In the model, approximate this as a 2.2 mm diameter core with effective volume resistivity `633 kOhm-cm`.
- Advantages: no ferrite discs to cut, no ferrite-to-disc epoxy joints, soldering can be used instead, and the resistor is expected to be low noise while ferrite noise is suspect.
- Possible issue: stray capacitance may be significant if the winding/film structure occupies much of the resistor body. Counterpoint: the substrate is likely lower permittivity than ferrite, and ferrite relative permittivity is about 13, so the MELF option may still have lower capacitance.

Resistor-voltage-stress correction:

- Do not size the MELF resistor or resistor chain as if the full 6 kV detector-bias supply appears across it in steady operation.
- Detector current is below 1 nA, so the resistor drop is approximately `I * R`; for 10 Mohm this is about 10 mV at 1 nA.
- Full-bias breakdown is a dielectric/spacing and pulse-energy issue. If a breakdown happens, the available energy is roughly `0.5 C V^2`, usually with pF-scale capacitance.
